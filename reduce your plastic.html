Reduce Your Plastic

Help protect our rivers and oceans by reducing plastic waste.

Why Reduce Plastic?

Plastic pollution is one of the most significant threats to our rivers and oceans. Every year, millions of tons of plastic waste end up in our rivers, harming wildlife and polluting our natural ecosystems. By reducing our use of plastic, we can help protect the environment, save wildlife, and promote sustainability.

How Plastic Affects Our Rivers

Plastic waste is not only unsightly, but it also disrupts the delicate balance of our ecosystems. Here's how plastic affects rivers:

  • Pollutes water: Plastic waste can leach toxic chemicals into water, harming aquatic life.
  • Endangers wildlife: Animals can mistake plastic for food, leading to suffocation, starvation, or injury.
  • Disrupts habitats: Plastic can block rivers and streams, affecting aquatic plant life and fish populations.

Practical Steps to Reduce Plastic Waste

Here are simple yet effective ways to reduce your plastic consumption and help clean our rivers:

  • Use reusable bags: Carry your own cloth or reusable bags when shopping.
  • Reduce single-use plastics: Avoid using disposable plastic cutlery, straws, and cups.
  • Choose products with minimal packaging: Opt for items with recyclable or no plastic packaging.
  • Recycle properly: Sort your waste and recycle plastics to prevent them from reaching the rivers.
  • Support eco-friendly brands: Choose companies that prioritize sustainable and plastic-free alternatives.

Take Action Today!

Your actions matter! By making small changes, you can have a big impact on reducing plastic waste and keeping our rivers clean.
